Study in Japan from Nepal

Your complete roadmap to higher education in Japan – From Kathmandu to Tokyo

Namaste and welcome to Apex Consultancy. Japan has become the top destination for Nepali students seeking quality education and robust career paths. This guide outlines the exact process to help you successfully transition to life in Japan.

Why Nepali Students Choose Japan?

Japan offers a unique combination of academic rigor and cultural familiarity. Key benefits include:

  • Global Recognition: Degrees from Japanese institutions are respected by employers across Asia and the West.
  • Scholarship Opportunities: MEXT, JASSO, and various private foundations offer generous support to diligent Nepali scholars.
  • Part-time Work (Arubaito): Students are legally allowed to work 28 hours/week, helping cover living expenses.
  • Path to Residency: Clear pathways exist for transitioning from a student visa to a work visa after graduation.

Academic Pathways

Most Nepali students choose one of these two routes:

1. Language School Foundation (Most Popular)

Spend 12–24 months mastering the language. Once you achieve N2 proficiency, you can transition into specialized vocational colleges (Senmon Gakko) or Universities. We represent top-tier schools in Tokyo, Osaka, and Fukuoka.

2. Direct University Entry

For students with high JLPT/NAT scores or those seeking English-medium programs (EJU/IELTS required). This is ideal for those pursuing Engineering, IT, or Global Business.

Our 5-Step Success Roadmap

Step 1: Free Profile Assessment

We analyze your academic background (+2/Bachelor’s) and financial capacity to find the school that fits your goals.

Step 2: Japanese Language Training

Enroll in our NAT/JLPT preparation classes in Kathmandu. A minimum of 150 hours of study is generally required for your application.

Step 3: Document Compilation & Submission

We guide you through the complex paperwork: Nata Pramanit (Relationship), Income Sources, and Property Valuation. Accuracy here is why our approval rate is so high.

Step 4: COE (Certificate of Eligibility)

Your documents are sent to Japanese Immigration. The wait time is typically 2–3 months. We keep you updated throughout the process.

Step 5: Visa Interview & Departure

Once the COE arrives, we prepare you for the Embassy interview in Panipokhari and host a pre-departure orientation to help you settle in Japan.

Financial Planning for Parents

Understanding the investment is crucial for Nepali families:

  • Total Initial Investment: Expect NPR 12,00,000 to 18,00,000 (includes tuition, processing, and airfare).
  • Bank Balance: A balance certificate of roughly NPR 20–25 Lakhs is usually required to show financial stability.
  • Safety First: Japan remains one of the world's safest nations, offering peace of mind to parents back home.
